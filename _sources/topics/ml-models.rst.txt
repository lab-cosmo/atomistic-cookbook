Machine learning models
=======================

This section contains recipes that concern the training of machine-learning
models, or the pre-processing of data to optimize the model architecture or
data.

    .. grid-item::
        .. card:: MD using direct-force predictions with PET-MAD
            :link: ../examples/pet-mad-nc/pet-mad-nc
            :link-type: doc
            :text-align: center
            :shadow: md

            .. image:: ../examples/pet-mad-nc/images/thumb/sphx_glr_pet-mad-nc_thumb.png
                :alt: Evaluating forces as a direct output of a ML model accelerates their evaluation, by up to a factor of 3 in comparison to the traditional approach that evaluates them as derivatives of the interatomic potential. Unfortunately, as discussed e.g. in this preprint, doing so means that forces are not conservative, leading to instabilities and artefacts in many modeling tasks, such as constant-energy molecular dynamics. Here we demonstrate the issues associated with direct force predictions, and ways to mitigate them, using the generally-applicable PET-MAD potential. See also this recipe for examples of using PET-MAD for basic tasks such as geometry optimization and conservative MD.
                :class: gallery-img
